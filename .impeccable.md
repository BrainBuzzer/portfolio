## Design Context

### Users

Multi-audience portfolio for Aditya Giri — fellow engineers who read code and appreciate craft, recruiters scanning for signal, and potential clients/investors evaluating competence and trustworthiness. The site must work equally well for a senior engineer digging into project details and a hiring manager spending 30 seconds on a first pass. Context: professionals browsing during the workday, often on laptops, sometimes on phones between meetings.

### Brand Personality

Technical, opinionated, grounded. The voice is direct and confident without being arrogant — someone who builds serious systems and has clear takes on how software should be made, but doesn't need to shout about it. The site should feel like talking to a sharp engineer who's thoughtful about their work, not a personal brand performing for an audience.

### Aesthetic Direction

Editorial / personal-site-influenced — inspired by Tom MacWright and Maggie Appleton's approach: writing-first, personality-forward, thoughtful without being precious. Clean but not sterile; warm but not soft. Avoid the "serious builder" dark-mode-technical aesthetic (Linear/Vercel) — that's a valid look but it's become the default for engineers and reads as generic. Instead, aim for something that feels like a well-considered personal space: a digital workshop with good lighting and organized shelves, not a control room with glowing screens.

- **Theme**: Both light and dark (keep current toggle). Dark mode should feel calm and focused, not neon-cyberpunk. Light mode should feel warm and readable.
- **Anti-references**: No overly flashy or trendy effects. No 3D, particles, or animation-for-animation's-sake. No startup hero sections or "hire me" CTAs. No Tailwind UI starter look.
- **Current palette foundation**: Zinc neutrals + teal accent is solid. The neon colors (cyan, purple, green) in tailwind.config.js feel at odds with the grounded personality — these should be reconsidered or removed.

### Design Principles

1. **Content-first clarity**: Typography, spacing, and hierarchy serve the words. The writing and projects are the star — design should amplify them, not compete with them.
2. **Grounded confidence**: Every design choice should feel intentional and considered, not trendy. Restraint is a feature. A few strong choices beat many weak ones.
3. **Warmth without softness**: Approachable and human, but still precise. Think "workshop" not "living room" — functional spaces that happen to be pleasant to be in.
4. **Editorial rhythm**: Varied layouts, considered whitespace, and visual pacing that rewards reading. Not a flat grid of identical cards — pages should breathe and have moments of emphasis.
5. **Accessible by default**: WCAG AA minimum. Proper contrast, keyboard navigation, screen reader support, and respect for reduced motion preferences. Accessibility is not a checkbox — it's how a grounded, technical person would build.
